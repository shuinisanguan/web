Chongqing's geographical environment

Chongqing is located in southwestern China, the upper reaches of the Yangtze River, in the transition zone between the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau and the plains of the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River.

Geographical advantage

Climate advantage

The climate is a subtropical monsoon humid climate. The average annual climate is around 18℃, the lowest temperature in winter is 6-8℃, the average summer temperature is 27-29℃, the total sunshine time is 1000-1200 hours, warm in winter and hot in summer, frost-free period Long, abundant rainfall, warm and humid, rainy and hot in the same season, the annual rainfall of 1000-1400 mm, especially at the turn of spring and summer, night rain, known as "Bashan night rain".

Terrain advantage

Chongqing has a vast territory, with rivers crisscrossing the area, and peaks and ridges are verdant. There is Daba Mountain in the north, Wu Mountain in the east, Wuling Mountain in the southeast, and Dalou Mountain in the south. The terrain generally slopes from north to south to the Yangtze River valley, with large ups and downs. The landform is dominated by hills and mountains, with large slopes and obvious stratification, with typical karst landscapes such as stone forests, peak forests, karst caves, and canyons. The main rivers are the Yangtze River, Jialing River, Wujiang River, Fujiang River, Qijiang River, Daning River and so on.

Location Advantage

It borders Hubei and Hunan to the east, Guizhou to the south, Sichuan to the west, and Shaanxi to the north. Chongqing is located in the core area of ​​the upper Yangtze River economic belt, the junction of China's east and west, and is a key development area for the Chinese government to implement the western development. It is one of the five major national central cities, and is also a national historical and cultural city, the economic center of the upper Yangtze River region, the financial center and the innovation center of the upper Yangtze River region

Chongqing Karst

The exposed area of ​​carbonate rocks in Chongqing is 32,000 square kilometers, accounting for 38.9% of the total area of ​​Chongqing. Mainly Wulong Karst and Jinfo Mountain Karst. Among them, Wulong Karst is distributed in Wulong District and is divided into three parts: Tianshengsanqiao, Qingkou Tiankeng and Furong Cave.

Karst caves in Chongqing include Wulong Furong Cave, Fengdu Xueyu Cave, Zhangguan Water Cave in Yubei, and Wushan Laolong Cave.
